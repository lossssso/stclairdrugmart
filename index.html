Our pharmacist administers vaccines right in the pharmacy — no doctor's visit required. Walk-ins welcome; appointments recommended for busy periods.

Publicly Funded — Free with a Valid Ontario Health Card

🆕 New as of July 1, 2026: Ontario pharmacists can now administer the publicly funded Tdap, shingles, pneumococcal & RSV vaccines — get them right here at the pharmacy, no doctor's visit needed.

Influenza (Flu Shot)
FREE
All Ontarians 6 months+ — annual

Updated each fall. Recommended for seniors, children, pregnant individuals, and those with chronic conditions.

COVID-19
FREE
All Ontarians 6 months+ — updated doses as recommended

Ask our pharmacist whether you're due for an updated dose — eligibility updated regularly by Ontario Public Health.

Tdap (Tetanus · Diphtheria · Pertussis)
NEW JUL 1FREE
Adults — one dose in adulthood · Every pregnancy · Tetanus booster every 10 years

Protects against tetanus, diphtheria, and whooping cough (pertussis). Publicly funded once in adulthood, in every pregnancy (ideally 27–32 weeks to protect newborns), and as a booster every 10 years or after an injury.

Shingles (Shingrix)
NEW JUL 1FREE
Adults 65–70 — two-dose series

Highly effective at preventing shingles and post-herpetic neuralgia. Publicly funded for ages 65–70 who haven't had a funded dose; available privately outside that range.

Pneumococcal
NEW JUL 1FREE
Adults 65+ · Younger adults at high risk

Protects against pneumococcal pneumonia. Recommended for those with heart disease, diabetes, lung or kidney disease.

RSV
NEW JUL 1FREE
Adults 75+ · Adults 60+ at high risk · Long-term care residents

Single dose protecting against RSV-related lower respiratory illness. Publicly funded for older adults at highest risk; available privately for others 60+.

Travel & Private Vaccines
Hepatitis A & B (Twinrix)
PRIVATE / INSURANCE
Travellers · At-risk individuals

Recommended for travel to Asia, Africa, Latin America, and the Middle East. Many benefit plans cover travel vaccines.

Typhoid
PRIVATE / INSURANCE
Travellers to endemic regions

Recommended for South Asia, sub-Saharan Africa, and Latin America travel. ~2–3 years of protection.

Meningococcal (ACYW)
PRIVATE / INSURANCE
Travellers · Students

Recommended for sub-Saharan Africa travel, Hajj, and adult catch-up.

HPV (Gardasil 9)
PRIVATE / INSURANCE
Adult catch-up · Up to age 45

Protects against 9 HPV strains linked to cancers and genital warts. Many extended health plans cover this series.

    Privacy Policy

    Effective Date: July 1, 2026  ·  Last Updated: June 2026

    1. About Us

    St. Clair Drug Mart Pharmacy (“the Pharmacy”) is located at 1203 St. Clair Avenue West, Toronto, ON M6E 1B6. We are a health information custodian as defined under the Personal Health Information Protection Act, 2004 (PHIPA). Our Designated Manager and Privacy Officer is Elizabeth Da Silva, BScPharm, RPh. Contact: stclairdrugmart@gmail.com  ·  (416) 654-8181.

    2. What This Policy Covers

    This policy applies to personal health information (PHI) and other personal information we collect through this website and in the course of providing pharmacy services. It does not govern information collected directly by third-party platforms (see Section 6).

    3. Information We Collect

    • Pharmacy services: Name, date of birth, health card number, medication history, prescriptions, and other PHI necessary to dispense medications and provide clinical services.
    • Drug Interaction Checker: Medication names you enter are sent anonymously to the U.S. National Library of Medicine (NLM RxNav API). No name, health card, or identifying information is sent. This website does not store any information you enter in the checker.
    • Contact & inquiries: Name, email, and phone number when you contact us voluntarily.
    • Website usage: This website does not use tracking cookies, analytics cookies, or advertising cookies. No personal data is collected through cookies.

    4. Purposes of Collection

    We collect PHI only for the purposes of providing healthcare — dispensing prescriptions, clinical assessments, medication reviews, and pharmacist consultations — or as required by law. We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information.

    5. Legal Authority

    Collection, use, and disclosure of PHI is governed by PHIPA (Ontario) and, where applicable, the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA). We comply with Ontario College of Pharmacists standards for patient record retention and confidentiality.

    6. Third-Party Platforms

    PharmAssess: Online booking, refill requests, prescription transfers, and minor ailment assessments are processed through PharmAssess (Kroll Computer Systems Inc.), a secure pharmacy software platform. Information you submit through the PharmAssess portal is subject to their privacy policy. St. Clair Drug Mart Pharmacy is not responsible for the privacy practices of PharmAssess.

    NLM RxNav: The Drug Interaction Checker sends anonymous drug names only to the U.S. National Library of Medicine public API. No identifying information is transmitted.

    7. Disclosure

    We may disclose PHI without your consent only as permitted or required by PHIPA — for example, to another health information custodian involved in your circle of care, or as required by law. We will not disclose PHI to third parties for marketing purposes.

    8. Security

    We implement physical, administrative, and technical safeguards appropriate to the sensitivity of the information, including secure storage of patient records and restricted access by pharmacy staff.

    9. Your Rights

    • Request access to your PHI held by the Pharmacy.
    • Request correction of inaccurate PHI.
    • Withdraw consent where consent is the legal basis for collection or use.
    • File a complaint with the Information and Privacy Commissioner of Ontario.
