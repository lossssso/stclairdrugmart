A face-to-face or phone consultation covering all your prescriptions, over-the-counter medications, vitamins, and supplements. We identify interactions, duplications, and medications that may no longer be appropriate for you.

Eligible if taking 3 or more chronic medications

A specialized review for patients living with Type 2 diabetes. We assess your diabetes medications, discuss blood sugar targets, and help coordinate care with your healthcare team.

For patients with Type 2 diabetes on any medication

Coming home from the hospital? We'll compare your discharge medications with your existing prescriptions to prevent dangerous errors, duplications, or gaps in your care during this critical transition.

Following hospital discharge or a care transition

After a significant medication change or concern

Vaccines at Our Pharmacy

Our pharmacist is trained to administer vaccines right in the pharmacy — no doctor's visit required. We offer Ontario's publicly funded vaccines at no cost to eligible patients, as well as travel and private vaccines. Walk-ins welcome; appointments recommended for busy periods.

Publicly Funded — Free with a Valid Ontario Health Card
Influenza (Flu Shot)
FREE
All Ontarians 6 months and older — annual

Updated each fall to match circulating strains. Especially recommended for seniors, children, pregnant individuals, and people with chronic health conditions such as asthma, heart disease, or diabetes.

COVID-19
FREE
All Ontarians — updated doses as recommended

Ontario offers updated COVID-19 vaccines seasonally. Recommended doses vary by age and health status. Ask our pharmacist whether you're due for an updated dose — eligibility guidance is updated regularly by Ontario Public Health.

Shingles (Shingrix)
FREE
Adults 65 and older · Adults 50+ who are immunocompromised

Two-dose series (second dose 2–6 months after the first). Highly effective at preventing shingles (herpes zoster) and its painful long-term complication, post-herpetic neuralgia. Even if you've had shingles before, this vaccine is recommended.

Pneumococcal
FREE
Adults 65 and older · High-risk individuals at any age

Protects against pneumococcal pneumonia and invasive pneumococcal disease. High-risk groups include those with heart disease, diabetes, lung disease, kidney disease, liver disease, cochlear implants, or a compromised immune system.

RSV (Respiratory Syncytial Virus)
FREE
Adults 60 and older · Available seasonally (fall & winter)

A single dose that protects against RSV-related lower respiratory illness — a leading cause of hospitalization in older adults. Particularly important for those with chronic cardiorespiratory or immunocompromising conditions.

Travel & Private Vaccines
Hepatitis A & B
PRIVATE / INSURANCE
Travellers · At-risk individuals

Available individually or as Twinrix (combined A+B). Recommended for travel to many regions of Asia, Africa, Latin America, and the Middle East. Check your extended health plan — many benefit programs cover travel vaccines.

Typhoid
PRIVATE / INSURANCE
Travellers to endemic regions

Injectable typhoid vaccine recommended for travel to parts of South Asia, sub-Saharan Africa, and Latin America where typhoid fever is common. Provides approximately 2–3 years of protection.

Meningococcal (ACYW)
PRIVATE / INSURANCE
Travellers · Students · Adult catch-up

Protects against four strains of meningococcal bacteria. Recommended for travel to sub-Saharan Africa's "meningitis belt," Hajj pilgrimage, and for adults who did not receive the publicly funded school-program dose.

HPV (Gardasil 9)
PRIVATE / INSURANCE
Adult catch-up · Up to age 45

Protects against 9 strains of human papillomavirus linked to cervical, throat, anal, and other cancers, as well as genital warts. Effective even if partially vaccinated in the past. Many extended health plans cover this series.

Not sure which vaccines are right for you? Our pharmacist will review your immunization history, health conditions, and travel plans to give you personalized recommendations — at no charge.

    Privacy Policy

    Effective Date: July 1, 2026  ·  Last Updated: June 2026

    1. About Us

    St. Clair Drug Mart Pharmacy (“the Pharmacy”) is located at 1203 St. Clair Avenue West, Toronto, ON M6E 1B6. We are a health information custodian as defined under the Personal Health Information Protection Act, 2004 (PHIPA). Our Designated Manager and Privacy Officer is Elizabeth Da Silva, BScPharm, RPh. Contact: stclairdrugmart@gmail.com  ·  (416) 654-8181.

    2. What This Policy Covers

    This policy applies to personal health information (PHI) and other personal information we collect through this website and in the course of providing pharmacy services. It does not govern information collected directly by third-party platforms (see Section 6).

    3. Information We Collect

    • Pharmacy services: Name, date of birth, health card number, medication history, prescriptions, and other PHI necessary to dispense medications and provide clinical services.
    • Drug Interaction Checker: Medication names you enter are sent anonymously to the U.S. National Library of Medicine (NLM RxNav API). No name, health card, or identifying information is sent. This website does not store any information you enter in the checker.
    • Contact & inquiries: Name, email, and phone number when you contact us voluntarily.
    • Website usage: This website does not use tracking cookies, analytics cookies, or advertising cookies. No personal data is collected through cookies.

    4. Purposes of Collection

    We collect PHI only for the purposes of providing healthcare — dispensing prescriptions, clinical assessments, medication reviews, and pharmacist consultations — or as required by law. We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information.

    5. Legal Authority

    Collection, use, and disclosure of PHI is governed by PHIPA (Ontario) and, where applicable, the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA). We comply with Ontario College of Pharmacists standards for patient record retention and confidentiality.

    6. Third-Party Platforms

    PharmAssess: Online booking, refill requests, prescription transfers, and minor ailment assessments are processed through PharmAssess (Kroll Computer Systems Inc.), a secure pharmacy software platform. Information you submit through the PharmAssess portal is subject to their privacy policy. St. Clair Drug Mart Pharmacy is not responsible for the privacy practices of PharmAssess.

    NLM RxNav: The Drug Interaction Checker sends anonymous drug names only to the U.S. National Library of Medicine public API. No identifying information is transmitted.

    7. Disclosure

    We may disclose PHI without your consent only as permitted or required by PHIPA — for example, to another health information custodian involved in your circle of care, or as required by law. We will not disclose PHI to third parties for marketing purposes.

    8. Security

    We implement physical, administrative, and technical safeguards appropriate to the sensitivity of the information, including secure storage of patient records and restricted access by pharmacy staff.

    9. Your Rights

    • Request access to your PHI held by the Pharmacy.
    • Request correction of inaccurate PHI.
    • Withdraw consent where consent is the legal basis for collection or use.
    • File a complaint with the Information and Privacy Commissioner of Ontario.
